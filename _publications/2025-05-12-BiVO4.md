BiVO₄ is a leading photoanode for solar water splitting, but its oxygen-evolution performance depends strongly on electrolyte anions (SO₄²⁻, NO₃⁻, HCO₃⁻) and on surface hole polarons. This work benchmarks thermostats for ab initio molecular dynamics of liquid water in CP2K — showing that single-chain Nosé–Hoover (or CSVR) reliably holds the 330 K target while multi-thermostat and NVE settings fail — and presents preliminary PBE0 results for SO₄²⁻ adsorbed on a hole-polaron-bearing BiVO₄(010) slab. H* binding is site-sensitive (~0.2 eV) while OH* is not, and a systematic ±1 µB magnetic-moment flip between H* and OH* configurations suggests polaron transfer coupled to OER-intermediate identity.
